# Research Report Workflow

A plain-English recipe for producing a thorough, well-organized research report on any topic.

---

## Step 1 — Intake (Ask Before Researching)

Before doing any research, ask the user these clarifying questions. Wait for answers before proceeding.

**Scope**
- How broad or narrow should the coverage be? (e.g., overview only, deep-dive, specific angle)
- Is there a time horizon? (e.g., last 12 months, all-time, future outlook)
- Are there sub-topics to include or explicitly exclude?

**Audience**
- Who will read this? (e.g., personal use, a team, a client, a general audience)
- What level of technical depth is appropriate?
- Any assumed background knowledge?

**Purpose / What They Specifically Want to Know**
- What decision or goal is this report serving? (e.g., purchase decision, due diligence, curiosity)
- Are there specific questions that must be answered?
- Preferred output length? (e.g., quick summary vs. comprehensive deep-dive)
- Any preferred structure or sections?

---

## Step 2 — Plan (Show Before Executing)

After intake, briefly state:
- The research approach (what sources / angles will be covered)
- The planned report sections
- Any known gaps or limitations upfront

Get a quick confirm before proceeding.

---

## Step 3 — Research

Search thoroughly across multiple angles. For each major claim or data point:
- Note the source
- Note the date (flag anything older than 12 months)
- Cross-check key facts with a second source when possible

Cover at minimum:
- Core facts / background
- Recent developments
- Key data, statistics, or evidence
- Competing perspectives or counterpoints (where relevant)
- Practical implications or takeaways

---

## Step 4 — Synthesize & Organize

Before writing the report:
- Group findings into logical sections
- Identify the 3–5 most important takeaways
- Flag any contradictions or areas of uncertainty
- Cut anything that doesn't serve the user's stated purpose

---

## Step 5 — Write the Report

Follow the standard report format:

```
# [Topic] Research Report
**Date:** [today's date] | **Purpose:** [from intake] | **Audience:** [from intake]

---

## Overview
[2–4 bullet executive summary — most important findings]

---

## [Section 1]
...

## [Section 2]
...

## Key Takeaways
[Numbered list of 3–5 actionable or notable conclusions]

## Recommended Next Steps
[Numbered list of 2–4 concrete actions the reader should consider taking, based on the findings. Tailor to the stated purpose — e.g., for a purchase decision: compare vendors, request a trial; for due diligence: verify a claim, consult an expert.]

---

## Sources
[Cited links or references, dated where possible]
```

Rules:
- Bullets over paragraphs
- Tables for comparisons or structured data
- Bold key terms and numbers
- Keep it as short as it can be while still being complete

---

## Step 6 — Save Output

Save the finished report to: `output/[slug-topic]-report.md`

Confirm the file path to the user when done.
